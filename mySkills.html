Electrical & Computer Engineering

I have spent many hours devoting myself to the study of electrical systems and have sharpened many useful skills in this area including

  • Computer Architecture Design

    During my senior year at UofM a team of five students and I designed a synthesizable Out-of-Order RISC-V processor in System Verilog. This processor was based on the MIPS R10K scheme. For more details see the projects section of my website.

  • Controls System Design

    During my undergrad on a team of three students we designed a PID controller for which controlled the height of a levetating magnetic ball via current in an inductor.

  • Digital Signal Processing and Wireless Communications

    One of my favorite areas of Electrical Engineering is DSP and Wireless Communications. The mathematics are extremely interesting to me and I am always excited to learn more about the subject.

  • Digital Logic Design and FPGA programming

    Beyond my capstone project designing a processor in System Verilog, I also have done many projects using Verilog on FPGAs. Notable projects include a traffic light intersection simulator as well as an NES controller Interface.
